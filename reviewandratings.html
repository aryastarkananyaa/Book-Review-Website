REVIEW AND RATINGS

THE SECRET--By Rohnda Byrne

GENRE: SELF HELP
There has been too much hype about this book which made me pick it and I'm definitely not regretting. 'The Secret', is a wonderful effort done in discussing The law of attraction, a law according to which you attract the circumstances and experiences you go through, good or bad, be it in family, career, relationships or desires in general. To put it in simpler terms, it brings forth the universe as a wish granting factory which focussing on the energies you exhale, says "Your wish is my command", as described beautifully by the author. The law of attraction is a debatable issue and there's a lot left to discuss and argue about the same. Imagine somebody telling you that you can better your relationship or get your dream car by merely thinking or meditating about it, well that raises eyebrows. But there's another level on which this book functions. When it enlightens the readers about the authority they hold over their lives and circumstances, they are bound to be more positive and confident. They experience a power within themselves and this itself is a commendable task in itself which this book does. Overall, its a must read to widen your horizon of understanding of yourself and the universe but as any self help book, it won't work if you don't practice.
Rating- ⭐⭐⭐⭐

THE BOOK THIEF--By MARKUS ZUSAK

GENRE: HISTORICAL,DRAMA

The first and the only book that has ever managed to make me cry so much like this.s a person who doesnt find history intriguing, with this book, I found myself entangled within the social hierarchy of the Nazi period and felt deeply moved by the history of Germany for the first time. The Book Thief is indeed a spectacular portrayal of the Nazi Germany in history during The Second World War This is the story of Liesel Meminger, who comes to live with her foster parents, Hans and Rosa Hubermann at a time when Hitler was rising to power. Their lives are threatened when a Jew named Max Vandenberg turns up in their house asking for help. The Hubermanns agree to shelter him in their basement, and hence begins the friendship between Liesel and Max. Beautiful and heartbreaking, this book is not for those who prefer straightforward linear narratives. But if you love words, read it if you haven't already.
Rating- ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

THE WIND -UP BIRD CHRONICLE-- By Haruki Murakami

GENRE:MAGICAL REALISM,SUSPENSE

I Don't know whether I can make this review as objective as possible because I'm really a fan of Haruki Murakami. This is the fourth book I read and I really addicted to his writing style, story plot, and such. All books I read so far is basically combined 2-3 different dimensions that connected to each other, and this book is no exception. The Wind-up Bird Chronicle is about a married man, Toru Okada who's looking for the mystery about his wife, Kumiko Okada who's suddenly disappeared. Kumiko doesn't give him any hints, but what he believes until the end is Kumiko trying to send him message as looking for help. Starting from the disappeared of the cat they're raised from the beginning of their marriage, the blocked alley behind their house, the vacant house that always give bad luck to whoever's live there, and several new peoples coming to their live. The plot is sometimes jump from one to another. This book combined back to the history of Japanese war in Manchukuo, the secret mission that only served 4 soldiers to the border of Siberia, and the cruelty of Russian soldiers after the defeated of Japanese as US dropped bomb to Hiroshima in 1945. The Wind-up Chronicle is actually about the sound of the bird that only can be hear of several people, those be able to move to another dimension as they sleep. All the plotlines are mixed up and confusing in a good way, all the mysteries aren't going to give you any hint, but to follow the path as you read this book. 606 pages give the exact amount of satisfaction for me.
Rating- ⭐⭐⭐⭐

I haven’t read such a fine novel in a long, long time! I can hardly believe that it’s a translation . Recommended only for the old souls. The museum of innocence definitely an exhibit in my museum. SAPIENS: A Brief History Of Humankind-- By Yuval Noah Harari

GENRE:EDUCATIONAL

I was very disappointed in parts of this book. It starts out well-- the overview of human-prehistory is quite decent, and I learned some new things, esp. about human impacts on biodiversity. The only parts of this book that really grabbed my attention were the chapters on early humankind, and especially the interaction between Homo Sapiens and other Homo species. The rest of it is a very pedestrian and basic journey through some aspects of human history, with the author making a lot of sweeping assertions and tending towards a rather vague and disembodied explanation of things like culture, money, etc. These sort of general explanations might be good for someone new to the study of history, but the reader should beware that they don't get taken in too much by the author's often simplistic and one-sided explanation of these concepts. The unifying theme of it being "a history of humankind", focusing on Homo Sapiens as a species, often disappears and it becomes a general plod through whatever aspects of history the author is interested in. More on Homo Sapiens' relations with other species and with nature might have been good.
Rating- ⭐⭐⭐
